# Cover Letter Template

**Word count target**: 250-400 words.
**Structure**: Hook → Alignment → Value → CTA.

This template is intentionally generic. Replace bracketed placeholders with real content. Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences each). Avoid clichés.

---

[Your Name]
[Your City, Country]
[Your Email] | [Your Phone] | [Your LinkedIn]

[Date — YYYY-MM-DD]

[Hiring Manager Name (if known) or "Hiring Team"]
[Company Name]

Re: [Exact Job Title from posting]

---

## Hook (1-2 sentences)

Reference a specific company news item, initiative, or commitment. Show you know what they're doing this quarter, not five years ago.

> "[Company]'s [recent specific initiative — e.g., $X commitment to Y, partnership with Z, launch of Product W] represents exactly the kind of [transformation / scale-up / market entry] I've spent my career enabling."

## Alignment (2-3 sentences)

Connect your specific experience to their needs. Pull one or two flagship achievements from your master-experience YAML — quantified, relevant, and credible.

> "At [Past Employer], I [specific accomplishment with metric — e.g., managed $X program across Y countries, generating Z in revenue]. My [credential or distinctive trait — e.g., PMP certification + experience orchestrating multi-stakeholder alignment on (flagship project)] directly parallels the [program-management / product / operations] demands of [Company]'s [initiative]."

## Value (2-3 sentences)

What unique value do you bring? Pull from your `config/user-profile.yaml` `value_props[]`. Mention regional expertise if it's relevant to the role.

> "As a [credibility anchor 1 — e.g., 2x AI/ML founder] now [credibility anchor 2 — e.g., based in your target region], I bring both the [trait A — e.g., entrepreneurial drive to innovate] and the [trait B — e.g., enterprise rigor to deliver at scale]. My deep familiarity with [region / industry / customer set] positions me to navigate the unique stakeholder dynamics of [Company]'s [initiative]."

## CTA (1-2 sentences)

Specific, low-friction next step. Confidence without presumption.

> "I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss how my background can contribute to [Company]'s [specific goal]. I'm available at your convenience."

---

Best regards,
[Your Name]

---

## What NOT to do (avoid these)

- Generic openers: "I am writing to express my interest in the role of..."
- Unsubstantiated fits: "I believe I would be a great fit because..."
- Resume rehashes: don't restate your CV in paragraph form — the cover letter should add narrative, not repeat bullets.
- Self-focus: "I'm looking for a role where I can grow / develop / be challenged." Reframe around the company's needs, not yours.
- Empty enthusiasm: "I am very excited about this opportunity!" — show it through specifics, don't claim it.
- Hedging language: "I think I could maybe contribute to..." — be definite.
- Emojis or casual tone (unless the company's culture explicitly invites it).

## What TO do

- Reference a specific, recent company event (last 60-90 days).
- Include at least one quantified achievement from your resume.
- Mention regional expertise when relevant.
- Show concrete knowledge of the company's current situation.
- Keep it under 400 words.
- Read it aloud — if a sentence feels stilted, rewrite it.

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] 250-400 words
- [ ] Specific company news / initiative referenced in the hook
- [ ] At least one metric from the resume in the alignment section
- [ ] Unique value proposition clearly stated
- [ ] Specific CTA with availability
- [ ] No "I believe I would be a great fit" clichés
- [ ] No generic openers
- [ ] Read aloud and flowing
- [ ] Matched to the right resume variant
