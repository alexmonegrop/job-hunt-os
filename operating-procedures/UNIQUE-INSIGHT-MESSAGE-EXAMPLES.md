# Unique Insight Outreach Message Examples

**Document type**: Reference. For automatic enforcement, see `.claude/rules/02-message-quality-standards.md`.

**Purpose**: 8 worked message examples (70-90 words each) demonstrating the 6-part formula. All examples use **fictional companies and people** for illustration.

> NOTE: replace fictional names with your real targets. The structure and rhythm are the lesson, not the specifics.

## The Formula

Structure (70-90 words):
1. News hook (5-10 words)
2. Unique insight (20-30 words)
3. Evidence / pattern (10-15 words)
4. Your credibility (10-15 words)
5. Geographic flexibility (5-10 words)
6. Soft ask (10-15 words)

## Example 1: AI Company Expanding to a New Region

**Target**: Head of International Expansion at fictional AI startup "Nimbus AI"
**News**: Just announced [target country] office opening

**Subject**: [Target country] AI Expansion — Why 3 Competitors Stalled

```
Hi Sarah,

Congrats on the [target city] office announcement.

Counterintuitive pattern: in [target country], AI companies succeed by starting with
government, not enterprise. The private sector waits for government validation.
[Big AI Co] learned this after wasting 18 months on enterprise sales.

I navigated this exact pathway at [Past Co] with [flagship gov client] and several
ministries. Currently in [user city], frequently in [target city].

15 minutes to share the government-first playbook?

Best,
[user]
```

**Word count**: 72
**Insight**: government-first approach (counter to typical B2B strategy)
**Evidence**: a competitor's 18-month mistake
**Credibility**: past role + flagship client

## Analysis: What Makes These Work

### Common elements
1. **Specific, timely news reference** (shows you're paying attention)
2. **Counter-intuitive insight** (challenges their assumptions)
3. **Concrete evidence** (competitor failure or success)
4. **Relevant credibility** (your parallel experience)
5. **Geographic availability** (removes logistical objection)
6. **Value-focused ask** (about helping them, not getting a job)

### The psychology
- Creates immediate value exchange
- Positions you as insider with special knowledge
- Makes them curious about what else you know
- Frames conversation as advisory, not job-seeking
- Demonstrates deep market understanding

### Differentiators
- Not generic ("I'd love to connect")
- Not needy ("Looking for opportunities")
- Not presumptuous ("I can help you")
- Not vague ("Extensive experience")

## Templates for Different Scenarios

### When competitor failed
"[Competitor] tried this and failed because [specific reason]. The solution isn't [obvious approach], it's [counter-intuitive approach]."

### When pattern exists
"Pattern I've observed: [X]% of [industry] companies fail at [specific point] because [non-obvious reason]."

### When timing matters
"The next [time period] determines success. Most optimise for [wrong metric], but [right metric] is what matters."

### When geography matters
"What works in [other region] fails in [target region] because [specific regional difference]."

## A/B Testing

Test different angles week-by-week. Track:
- Which insights get responses
- Which get "tell me more"
- Which lead to calls
- Which get forwarded internally

## Red Flags to Avoid

Don't:
- Give away the entire solution
- Make insights generic or obvious
- Sound like you're lecturing
- Make claims without evidence
- Focus on technology when problem is human
- Assume they don't know (frame as pattern)

Do:
- Leave them wanting more
- Be specific to their situation
- Share as peer / advisor
- Back up with concrete examples
- Focus on hidden human dynamics
- Frame as "pattern I've observed"

---

*The goal: demonstrate you understand something about their challenge that others don't — and that this understanding is valuable enough for 15 minutes of their time.*

**Key metric**: response rate with insights should be 20-30% vs 10-15% without.
